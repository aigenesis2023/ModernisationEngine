System Architecture & Safety Objectives

Understanding
the Hazards

Key Technical Risks

flash_on ELECTRIC SHOCK
Direct contact with live components can lead to thermal burns and cardiac arrest. HV systems in EVs are isolated from the chassis, meaning a double-fault is usually required for a shock hazard.
local_fire_department THERMAL RUNAWAY
Chemical reaction within battery cells leading to rapid temperature increase and venting of toxic gases. Can be triggered by impact, overcharging, or short-circuits.
smoke_free ARC FLASH
High energy discharge through the air between conductors, causing explosive pressure and intense ultraviolet light.

CRITICAL_RESPONSE

What is Electric Shock?

Electric shock occurs when the body becomes part of an electrical circuit. In an EV context, this typically involves a DC current flow which can cause continuous muscle contraction (the "no-let-go" effect).

Severity Factor 01

Voltage Potential

Higher voltage increases the current's ability to overcome skin resistance.

emergency Emergency Procedure

  1. 01

    DO NOT TOUCH the victim directly if they are still in contact with the source.

  2. 02

    Use a non-conductive rescue hook to pull the victim away.

  3. 03

    Alert emergency services and specify "HIGH VOLTAGE INCIDENT".

Charging Terminology

bolt
Type 2 Connector

The standard AC charging plug for European EVs. Supports single-phase and three-phase charging up to 43kW.

speed
CCS (Combo 2)

Combined Charging System. Adds two large DC pins to the Type 2 base for rapid charging up to 350kW.

power
CHAdeMO

A DC fast-charging standard developed in Japan. Uses a distinct circular connector and bidirectional power flow.
