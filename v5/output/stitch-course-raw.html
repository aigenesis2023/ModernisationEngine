The Core Mandate

Why Safety Matters

Safety is not merely a set of rules; it is a shared culture of vigilance. In every workplace, from the corporate office to the industrial floor, the primary objective remains the same: ensuring every team member returns home in the same condition they arrived.

By understanding the fundamentals of hazard identification and risk management, we transform from passive observers into active guardians of our collective well-being.

Hierarchy of Controls

The Hierarchy of Controls is a system used in industry to minimize or eliminate exposure to hazards. It is a widely accepted system that is promoted by numerous safety organizations.

verified

Most effective methods involve complete removal of the hazard.

warning

PPE is considered the last line of defense, not the first.

The Five Levels of Hazard Control

Elimination expand_more
Physically remove the hazard. This is the most effective control. If a task is not necessary, or the hazard can be removed entirely, this must be the first choice.
Substitution expand_more
Replace the hazard with something less dangerous. For example, using a non-toxic chemical instead of a corrosive one.
Engineering Controls expand_more
Isolate people from the hazard through physical barriers, guards, or ventilation systems.

Types of Workplace Hazards

Physical Hazards

Physical hazards are environmental factors that can cause harm to a worker even without touching them. They are common in almost all work settings.

Key Terminology

Hazard

A potential source of harm or adverse health effect on a person or persons.

Risk

The likelihood that a person may be harmed or suffer adverse health effects if exposed to a hazard.

Near Miss

An unplanned event that did not result in injury but had the potential to do so.
