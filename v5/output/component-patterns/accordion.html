The Five Levels of Hazard Control

Elimination expand_more
Physically remove the hazard. This is the most effective control. If a task is not necessary, or the hazard can be removed entirely, this must be the first choice.
Substitution expand_more
Replace the hazard with something less dangerous. For example, using a non-toxic chemical instead of a corrosive one.
Engineering Controls expand_more
Isolate people from the hazard through physical barriers, guards, or ventilation systems.
