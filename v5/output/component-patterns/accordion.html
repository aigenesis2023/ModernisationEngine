The Five Levels of Hazard Control

01. Elimination expand_more
Physically remove the hazard. This is the most effective control measure. If the hazard is not there, the risk is zero.
02. Substitution expand_more
Replace the hazard with something less dangerous. For example, replacing a toxic chemical with a non-toxic alternative.
03. Engineering Controls expand_more
Isolate people from the hazard. This involves physical changes to the workplace, like machine guards or ventilation systems.
04. Administrative Controls expand_more
Change the way people work. Includes training, signs, and procedures that manage how the exposure occurs.
05. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) expand_more
Protect the worker with equipment. This is the last line of defense and relies on correct usage and fit.
