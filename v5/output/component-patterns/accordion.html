The Five Levels of Hazard Control

1. Elimination expand_more
Physically remove the hazard. This is the most effective control measure. If the task or material isn't necessary, remove it entirely.
2. Substitution expand_more
Replace the hazard with something less dangerous. For example, using water-based paint instead of solvent-based paint.
3. Engineering Controls expand_more
Isolate people from the hazard. Installing physical barriers, ventilation systems, or machine guarding.
4. Administrative Controls expand_more
Change the way people work. Training, procedure changes, and signage are examples of administrative controls.
5. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) expand_more
Protect the worker with equipment. Hard hats, gloves, and earplugs. This is the last line of defense.
