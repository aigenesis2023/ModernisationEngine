Financial Risk Management Fundamentals

A comprehensive course on understanding and managing financial risk in modern organisations. Covers the core frameworks, tools, and strategies that risk professionals use to identify, assess, and mitigate financial threats.

Key topics to cover:
1. What financial risk is — market risk, credit risk, liquidity risk, operational risk, the difference between systematic and unsystematic risk
2. Risk identification and assessment — how organisations discover and quantify exposure, risk registers, heat maps, scenario analysis
3. Value at Risk (VaR) and other quantitative measures — how banks and firms calculate potential losses, confidence intervals, limitations of models
4. Hedging strategies — derivatives (futures, options, swaps), natural hedging, when to hedge vs when to accept risk
5. Credit risk management — counterparty risk, credit scoring, collateral, credit default swaps, lessons from the 2008 financial crisis
6. Regulatory frameworks — Basel III/IV, stress testing requirements, capital adequacy, how regulation shapes risk behaviour
7. Building a risk culture — embedding risk awareness across the organisation, the role of the Chief Risk Officer, risk appetite statements, real-world case studies of risk failures and successes

Target audience: Finance professionals, risk analysts, and business managers
Difficulty: Intermediate (assumes basic financial literacy)
Estimated duration: ~45 minutes
